User Interface Design: Open House

For my Human Interface Design class (HCDE 455) I worked with 3 other students to design an interface that's goal was to make interior design easier. The class walked us through different phases, each with its own deliverable, and ended with a complete, functional prototype: a mobile app called Open House.

Phase 1: Prospectus Prepared a four page document outline our project.

  • Team Identity: Name and motto
  • Product functionality: What is it supposed to do? What problem does it solve?
  • Features (preliminary): What features does it have and how much can it cost?
  • User population: Who will use it and why? In what environment will they use it? What is the market size?
  • Resources: People in the team + their skills
  • Constraints: Time to accomplish project
  • Project Organization/Schedule

Phase 2: Project Characterization In this phase, we conducted user research to better understand the problem. We also did some scholarly research on 3D interaction techniques and Virtual Reality.

Each team member interviewed someone. I interviewed my cousin for this phase, who received a degree in interior design. Our results revealed that one of the greatest challenges about interior design was finding inexpensive, quality furniture and being able to envision furniture (both online and in store) within your actual space. Our team wanted to focus on the latter issue: creating an interface that could assist the user in envisioning furniture in their space without the furniture physically being there.

Phase 3A: Persona

Phase 3B: Storyboards We sketched up a storyboard with our persona, Christian. The story was that he wanted to incorporate a sand castle as an accent piece in his living room but his wife wasn't having it. He used Open House to digitally redecorate his living room to have a beach theme and used it to persuade his wife to redecorate their living room in real life.

Here are two of the eleven scenes we drew.

Phase 4: Task Analysis

For this phase, we identified the user flow for three tasks we planned on including in our interface. We created a system map for the interface and made flow charts for each task. This is one of those flowcharts, created with Lucidchart.

Phase 5: Flipbook Field Test Report AKA Paper Prototype

This was our first prototype, constructed with cardstock.

Phase 6: Mockups

For this phase, one of our team members coded our design into a mobile app prototype. Open House features Augmented Reality to let the user view 3D models of furniture through their iPhone camera. We included a social aspect too, letting users share their designs and follow others for inspiration; we incoporated this because our user interviews reported value in others' opinions for interior design.

Phase 7: Prototype Field Test Report

We ran some user tests with our mockup and made changes to our design based on the results. Originally we included both virtual realitiy and augmented reality to our design, but our user tests showed that the average user had trouble differentiating how to get to one or the other on our app. Also, the augmented reality feature seemed a lot more popular so we decided to narrow our scope and focus on AR more. We also changes button and icon designs to be more clear.

Phase 8: Final Report

Our final deliverable was a team report documenting the entire process.

GAMER Group

This was my final project for Client-Side Web Development (INFO 343). It was my first time working with a group on one coding project and it was also my first time working with a client.

Our client, GAMER Group, is a research group here at UW. Their big project is to create a catalogue to document video games. Their intention is to be the go-to source for video game information, like how IMDb is the most reputable source for movie information.

They needed a website that could be used for gaming companies to document their video games. So we created this website with a large, multi-page form to submit into the database.

Users could see the video games they submitted in the past and edit their user information as well.

I did the bulk of the CSS3 styling and created the multi-page architecture. This project was one of the most challenging I ever worked on, because I had never coded with other people before and had never used GitHub in that way either. Working with a client showed me how important it is to have good commmunication is on a project like this. It was a rewarding experience.
